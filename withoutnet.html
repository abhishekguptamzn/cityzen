SECTION 01

The Dead Zone Problem

When disaster strikes, communication is the first casualty. Existing systems fail exactly when they are needed most.

📡

Cellular Networks Fail

During earthquakes, floods, or storms, cell towers lose power or get overloaded. SMS and internet go down.

  • Towers require grid power and fiber optics
  • Networks congestion during panic
  • Repair takes days or weeks
💸

Satellite & Pagers are Expensive

Issuing satellite phones or custom pagers to every citizen is financially impossible for any municipality.

  • High hardware cost per user
  • Monthly subscription fees
  • Citizens must carry extra devices
SECTION 02

The Aether Solution

Use the government's ability to build a long-range radio network, and bridge it to the devices citizens already carry in their pockets via Bluetooth.

📻

Long-Range LoRa Backbone

The city installs cheap LoRa radio transmitters. They broadcast encrypted text alerts over 433MHz radio, immune to internet outages and cell tower failures.

  • Travels up to 15km per node
  • Penetrates buildings and debris
  • Operates on solar/battery power
📱

Bluetooth BLE Bridge

Solar-powered "Bridge Hubs" placed in neighborhoods listen for the LoRa radio signal and instantly rebroadcast it as a Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) beacon to nearby smartphones.

  • Citizens just need a free app
  • App works with phone in airplane mode
  • Zero hardware cost for the public

→
STEP 03

Neighborhood Bridge

A $10 hub on a local pole catches the radio message and converts it to a Bluetooth BLE beacon.

→
STEP 04
📲

Citizen Phone

The offline app detects the BLE beacon, triggers a loud alarm, and displays the text on screen.

Why Bluetooth BLE? Every modern smartphone has BLE. It works even if the phone has no SIM card or is in Airplane Mode. The app runs a background service listening for a specific "Aether UUID" broadcasted by the hubs.
SECTION 04

The "Bridge Hub" Hardware

The core of the system. A minimalist, solar-powered device that translates LoRa radio waves into Bluetooth signals.

🧠

ESP32 Microcontroller

The brain. Handles LoRa SPI communication and broadcasts Bluetooth BLE simultaneously.

📶

LoRa RA-01 (433MHz)

Receives the long-range encrypted signal from the City Command repeaters.

☀️

Solar + 18650 Battery

Runs 24/7 off-grid. Survives total power blackouts for weeks.

🔧

Enclosed & Weatherproof

Mounted on light poles or roofs every 1-2 km in dense neighborhoods.

App Architecture

1. Background Scanner

Uses Android/iOS BLE APIs to scan for the specific Aether UUID every 2 seconds without draining battery.

2. Payload Decryptor

Once a hub is found, it reads the BLE manufacturer data payload (up to 255 bytes). AES-128 decryption runs locally.

3. Critical Notification

Bypasses phone's silent mode. Triggers a loud siren and displays the alert as a full-screen high-priority push notification.

4. Offline Cache

Stores last 10 messages in local SQLite DB so citizens can review them even if the hub moves out of range.

SECTION 07

Implementation Roadmap

A phased approach to deploy Project Aether safely and effectively.

Phase 1: Proof of Concept

Weeks 1-4

Build 3 prototype hubs. Develop the Android app. Test end-to-end message delivery from a laptop to a smartphone across a neighborhood. Measure BLE range in urban environments.

Phase 2: City Backbone

Months 2-3

Install solar-powered LoRa repeaters on 3-4 city-owned high points (water towers, city hall roof). Map coverage using RSSI data from a mobile testing rig.

Phase 3: Neighborhood Hubs

Months 4-5

Deploy 50 Bridge Hubs on light poles. Launch the citizen app publicly. Run a city-wide emergency drill comparing Aether's delivery rate to traditional SMS.

Phase 4: Full Integration

Month 6+

Integrate the Command Center software with existing emergency dispatch systems. Add multilingual support and accessibility features (vibration patterns for hearing impaired).
